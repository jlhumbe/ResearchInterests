Coastal Morphodynamics

1)Inlet Morphodynamics

Morphodynamics is a fairly crazy word if you're not familiar with it, but it can quite easily be broken down to derive its meaning:
a. morpho – meaning form or shape
b. dynamic – meaning change, particularly active change

Therefore morphodynamics simply refers to the active change in the shape of something. My work focuses on coastal morphodynamics, so the active change in shape of the coasts. My current work specifically looks at how the shape of the bottom changes around Oregon Inlet, a large and dynamics inlet system seperating Bodie Island from Pea Island of the Outer Banks, NC. We examine the evolution of the bottom using surface changes in wave shoalling processes as a proxy for the underlying bathymetry.
